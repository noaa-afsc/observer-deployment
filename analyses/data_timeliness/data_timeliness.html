Data Timeliness as an Evaluation Metric

2023-06-28

Calculating Data Timeliness

For most trips, I calculated data timeliness as the time between a trip ending and data being available to the Catch Accounting System. For trawl EM, I used offload end date instead of trip end date, as I was unable to link trip end date to trawl EM offloads. I used the time at which data were available to the CAS, rather than the time at which data were submitted to the AFSC, as I could measure the former more consistently across EM and observed strata. I used data from 2022 to calculate stratum-specific data timeliness.

Dashed red lines represent a data timeliness of one week, and the percentage shown for each stratum is the percentage of trips that had a timeliness of one week or less.

Creating a Function

I created a function that uses the stratum-specific proportion of trips that have a timeliness of one week or less to estimate the proportion of all trips within a data set (monitored and unmonitored) that have a timeliness of one week or less.

##    COVERAGE_TYPE     STRATA one_week_quantile
## 1:          FULL EM_TRW_EFP         0.5315789
## 2:          FULL       FULL         0.9944683
## 3:       PARTIAL     EM_HAL         0.0000000
## 4:       PARTIAL     EM_POT         0.0000000
## 5:       PARTIAL EM_TRW_EFP         0.2023810
## 6:       PARTIAL        HAL         0.9816514
## 7:       PARTIAL        POT         0.9636364
## 8:       PARTIAL        TRW         0.9785408

Unmonitored trips receive the same timeliness as monitored trips within a stratum, since data from monitored trips will be used to generate estimates for unmonitored trips. As an example, the function estimates that 0.7792 of trips in 2022 had a timeliness of one week or less.

evaluate_data_timeliness(data, strata_timeliness)
## [1] 0.7792

This can be compared to a fictitious data set in which all trips were in the HAL stratum:

evaluate_data_timeliness(copy(data)[, STRATA := "HAL"], strata_timeliness)
## [1] 0.9817
